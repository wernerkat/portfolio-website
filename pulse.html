Primary Direction

Our given project brief was general: Allow health-conscious individuals to log in to a responsive health and wellbeing portal that offers them features to support their health.

Role

UI/UX Designer

Client

5 Months

Tools Used

Background

While talking with a nurse practitioner, I heard the caregiver's perspective on the struggles her patients have with engagement in their health.

"They spend so much time on unimportant things, on their phones... and so little time on their wellbeing".

This was the catalyst for my desire to better understand this problem.
What tools might be able to support people with their healthcare, and if they already exist, why aren't they working?

Competitor Analysis

My analysis began as a very broad exploration of digital health product offerings.



Look deeper into my initial analysis here

Through this initial analysis, I realized this problem space was vast, and I needed guidance from users to discover needs that are still unmet through any existing health apps.

Surveys

I asked 20 people to complete surveys about how they currently engage with their health.

  • Nearly 70% of respondants felt that tracking health data was important to them.
  • 65% said that finding good healthcare was a barrier to better health.
The surveys brought clarity to the type of health app that might bring needed empowerment to users - a health charting app.

Interviews

Six people who already used at least one health or wellness app to track an aspect of their health were interviewed.

Interview Goals

1. Learn how people currently engage with health history and tracking health information
2. Learn common practices for scheduling healthcare appointments and receiving test results ect.
3. Find out which health and wellness apps and websites being used

Insights

  • Most people either use a paper filing system or rely on their doctor when they need historical health data
  • The way that users prefer to receive test results is highly variable
  • Even indivuals with complex health history do not want to put in the effort to track their health data
  • Finding a new doctor is highly stressful
  • There aren’t lots of regularly used health charting apps because they are poorly deigned

Feedback from interviews took me back to the analysis phase. Following the Double Diamond Stragety, I was able to narrow the scope and better understand the problem I was hoping to solve.

Aha Moment: Maybe thinking an app can make users want to be more engaged with their care is the wrong way to go about it. Healthcare is percceived as a chore by many people, not something they enjoy. A more impactful focus will be to make this daunting process of receiving care easier.

Targeted Hypothesis

By enabling users to find health and wellness care providers, schedule appointments and store their health data in one place we will see them more empowered through their health journey.

Personas

Healthcare is a universal topic. With Two key peronas encompassed a large range of potential users.

User Stories

  • As a person with a simple health question, I want to be able to get an answer from a healthcare provider without having to go into their office.
  • As a person in need of healthcare, I want to schedule an appointment with a doctor based on my availability for convenience.
  • As a person in need of healthcare, I want to see all of my options for a doctor near me so that I can select the doctor that I am most comfortable with.
  • As a person in need of healthcare, I want to search for providers my their specialization so that I can see the doctor that will help me with my issue.
User stories helped determine the most critical features to build out first.

Important Features:

  • Finding & booking care
  • Saving health data
  • Contacting a provider between appointments

Conclusion

The Pulse app is a tool to simplify an overly complicated process. It solves the problem of keeps health data in the hands of the true owners, the patients. It also facilitated easier finding and booking of doctors. With every aspect of a healthcare appointment in one place, patients can focus on their health instead of administrative hassles.

Discoveries

1. User feedback can change everything. They generally felt that healthcare is a chore, and what they need is a way to make it simpler, not necessarily a way to be more involved. Learning this shaped the entire direction for the app.
2. Phrasing and quick adaptation is very important when asking test questions. I had a few instances where my questions came off too ambiguous or too specific, and the data I received from those usability tests was not reliable. I learned how to get better results from my questions the more testing I completed.

Future Improvements

  • Emphasis on health data security & privacy
  • Concept the app for the provider’s side *This would likely have been built for release simultaneously, as it would be monetized via the provider paying to belong to the platform*
  • Integrations with Google Calendar and Apple Health
  • Deeper level of accessibility including voice control
